TURN 2 · THE NOTCHED METER, SCALED
One meter, 2 to a million
The fixed meter you liked, made to scale. It always fills left→right (no more flipping direction), and it always snaps to a real group value — every position you can land on is an actual possible outcome, so the number can never be a fraction the room can’t produce. The per-person notches only draw while they’re legible (≤ 15 others); above that they dissolve into quarter gridlines and the readout switches from “N of M” to a percentage. Drag it, and toggle the room size — watch it cross the threshold at 25.

TURN 1 · PREDICTION STEP
Four ways to fix the predict step
Every variant fixes the two observed problems: direction never flips (your answer always anchors left, more agreement is always rightward), and snapping is embraced as discrete choices instead of a slider fighting you. All are live — tap the “You said” chip to flip sides, and use the 3 / 7 / 23 toggle to change room size. The current design is on the far right for comparison.
1a
Count the people
No percent, no slider. Tap or drag across the heads, or use −/+ for one-at-a-time precision. Discrete by design — the snap IS the interaction.
